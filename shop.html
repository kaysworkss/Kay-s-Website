Before you ask

Frequently asked

Once your order ships, you'll receive an email with your tracking number. Nigerian orders ship via GIG Logistics — track at giglogistics.com with your waybill number. International orders ship via DHL or UPS — track on the carrier's site with the number in your email. Kay packs and ships on weekends, so tracking details typically arrive early the following week. If you have any questions about your order in the meantime, reach out via the Questions button and Kay will update you directly.
We accept cards via Flutterwave (international cards — Visa, Mastercard) and Paystack (Nigerian cards, bank transfer, USSD). We also accept cryptocurrency: ETH and stablecoins (USDC, USDT) on Ethereum, plus XTZ on Tezos. Card payments are processed securely; crypto payments are confirmed on-chain before your order is finalised. Choose your method at checkout.
Often, yes. Many pieces can be printed at a custom size beyond the standard options listed, depending on the source artwork's resolution. Use the Questions? button (bottom-right) or message us on WhatsApp with the piece and the size you have in mind, and we'll let you know what's possible and quote you a price.
International orders ship from Nigeria via DHL and UPS — chosen for their global reach and customs handling, typically delivering to most countries within a few business days. Prints ship flat in a rigid mailer; large prints ship rolled in a protective tube. Duties and import taxes are set by your country and are the buyer's responsibility — the carrier will contact you if anything is due on arrival. A per-region delivery rate is shown at checkout before you pay.
Yes. Nigerian orders ship via GIG Logistics to any state. If you'd prefer pickup at a nearby GIG station rather than your door, note it in the address field at checkout.
Limited-edition prints are produced in a fixed run and hand-numbered (e.g. 3 of 30). Once the run sells out, no more are made. The number remaining is shown on the product page, and the bag won't let you add more than are left.
Use the Questions? button in the bottom-right corner any time — send a message by email or reach us directly on WhatsApp. We reply within 24 hours.

How to measure yourself

Chest Measure around the fullest part of your chest, keeping the tape horizontal under your arms.
Waist Measure around your natural waistline, the narrowest part of your torso.
Hips Stand with feet together and measure around the fullest part of your hips.
Body length Measure from the top of your shoulder straight down to where you want the garment to end.
